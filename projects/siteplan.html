Site Plan: Personal Portfolio Website

Site Name

Site Name: DevPortfolio Pro

Reason: This name was selected because it clearly conveys the professional and developmental focus of the website. It signifies a platform where a developer's portfolio is showcased professionally, making it attractive to potential employers and clients.

Site Purpose

Site Purpose: The purpose of this site is to provide an online portfolio that showcases my professional skills, projects, and background. It aims to attract potential employers and clients by providing detailed information about my capabilities, experience, and contact information.

Scenarios

  • Scenario 1: A potential employer wants to see examples of my work to assess my technical skills.
    • Question: What are some projects that demonstrate your skills and experience?
  • Scenario 2: A potential client wants to know more about my background and professional qualifications.
    • Question: Can you provide a detailed biography and list of your skills and education?

Color Schema

  • Primary Colors:
    • Dark Blue (#2C3E50): Used for headers, navigation bar, and footer background.
    • Light Gray (#ECF0F1): Used for the background of content sections, forms, and accent areas.
  • Secondary Colors:
    • Green (#27AE60): Used for buttons, links, and highlights.
    • White (#FFFFFF): Used for text in dark backgrounds and form elements.

Typography

  • Primary Font: Montserrat
    • Usage: Headings, navigation bar, and footer.
  • Secondary Font: Open Sans
    • Usage: Body text, forms, and project descriptions.

Functional and Developmental Specifications

  1. Hosting: The website will be hosted on GitHub and rendered via GitHub Pages.
  2. Pages: The site will consist of at least four pages (Home, About, Projects, Contact) with a common navigation and theme.
  3. Content: The site will provide significant and relevant content that supports its purpose, including project descriptions and personal biography.
  4. Design: The design will be accessible, usable, visually appealing, and follow design principles.
  5. HTML & CSS: Pages will be structured with valid, standards-based HTML markup and styled with valid CSS.
  6. Responsiveness: The site will be responsive, adapting to both mobile and desktop views.
  7. Images: All images will be optimized, and lazy loading will be used.
  8. Forms: An HTML form will be included for the contact page, with built-in form validation.
  9. JavaScript: The site will include dynamic elements such as DOM interaction, event listeners, conditional branching, objects, arrays, array methods, template literals, and localStorage.
  10. Quality: The site will be free of spelling and grammatical errors.
